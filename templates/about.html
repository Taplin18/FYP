  • What is a Spatial Bloom Filter?
  • Location privacy is a growing issue in cybersecurity.
  • The spatial bloom filter is one of the many privacy-preserving protocols aimed at mitigating this threat to personal privacy.
  • The SBF is a data structure based on bloom filters, designed to store location information.
  • The protocol keeps the user's exact location hidden but allows the provider of the service to learn when the user is close to specific points of interest or inside predefined areas.
  • At the same time, the points and areas of interest remain oblivious to the user.
  • The Cork dataset
  • There are four Areas of Interest marked on the map of Cork and each area has a set of coordinates associated with it.
  • A geospatial grid is placed on the map which allows the coordinates that are extracted to be accurate to 0.0001 of a degree.
  • An example of an Area of Interest is shown below.
  • When the dataset is imported, it is the Area of Interest number (1, 2, 3 or 4) that is placed in the spacial bloom filter.
  • Each coordinate is placed in the SBF three times as there is three hash functions run.
  • As there is 1,374 values placed into the SBF, the Area of Interest number can be overwritten by the new values that are being inserted.
  • Check the Test Data tab for coordinates that are now incorrect as their Area of Interest value has been overwritten after importing the cork.csv.
Area of Interest
  • Example of the geospacial grid with Area of Interest 1 highlighted.
  • The coordinates for are taken from the bottom-left of each cell within the area.
  • Example of a coordinate: 51.8954#-8.4772.
  • There is 458 coordinates in total between the four Areas of Interest.
  • Check Results
  • When querying the spacial bloom filter, the result is which Area of Interest it belongs to.
  • There will be three Area of Interest results as the coordinate was placed in the SBF three times as there is three hash functions in the hash family.
  • The lowest Area of Interest value is the answer as cells can be overwritten.
  • If any of the three results are zero, then the value queried is not in the SBF.
  • As cells can be overwritten, it is possible that the wrong Area of Interest is returned.
